Best Builds

Page Filter

Useful for big overview pages when you want one item fast.

  • The strongest Sailor Piece builds are layered across weapons, melees, races, clans, traits, powers, spec passives, titles, accessories, artifacts, runes, and account-wide upgrades.
  • There is no single universal perfect stat total because sword damage, melee damage, and luck farming all use different endgame slot choices and artifact rolls.
  • This page focuses on the current theoretical cap loadouts and cap assumptions from the wiki's own equipment, title, rune, artifact, and upgrade pages.
  • The newest late-game build targets now assume higher system caps such as Armament level 100, Observation level 50, Conqueror level 35, Skill Tree 490 SP, maxed rune stats, and full Crystal Defense upgrades.
  • This page treats maxed Haki, Ascension, Skill Tree, Blessings, Enchants, Tower upgrades, Boss Rush upgrades, Crystal Defense upgrades, and Artifact upgrades as part of the endgame target, not as optional extras.
  • Full Crystal Defense contributes another +25% Damage, +15% Crit Damage, +5% Crit Chance, +37.5% HP, and +15% Luck to the cap route.
  • Using the newer Sea 2 sword, melee, title, relic, power, Demon Wing accessory, and Crystal Defense assumptions, the current broad theoretical damage ceiling now counts closer to about +1,192% to +1,207%, with the last roughly +60% to +75% of that total still moving around based on how perfect your artifact damage mains and substats really are.
  • The in-game Luck panel counts Base +100% first, adds the visible source segments, then applies 2x Luck gamepass and 2x global event multipliers to that whole panel total.
  • The older near-cap luck comparison sheet still lands around +425% panel Luck, but once max Crystal Defense Luck is added the current full cap reads closer to about +440% before boosts, about +880% with one 2x Luck boost, and about +1,760% with both the 2x Luck gamepass and a 2x global luck event.
  • Current health benchmarks now point closer to about 60,200,000 HP on real max-level HP routes once full Crystal Defense HP is layered in, so the older 18,000,000, 25,000,000, 32,000,000, and even 38,000,000 to 40,000,000 placeholder estimates are no longer used on the build totals.
  • Utility rows now show base and 2x gamepass values separately; the current confirmed utility benchmark is +100% EXP, +205% Money, and +205% Gems before gamepasses.

How to use this page

  • Use the Max Sword Damage path if your main damage source is your weapon line.
  • Use the Max Melee Damage path if your main damage source is your fighting style line.
  • Use the Max Luck Farming path when your goal is drop value, reroll farming, tower farming, and boss loops.
  • Treat this page as a theoretical cap page with current in-game stat-panel benchmarks where we have screenshots, because exact totals still move around with artifact mains, substats, stat rerolls, gamepasses, and active global events.
  • This page uses the wiki's own item and system tables first, so accessory, aura, rune, title, and enchant assumptions come from the current local wiki data.
  • Treat Ice Queen or Dragon Goddess as the current broad sword cap lane on this wiki, while The World and Cosmic Being are the main Sea 2 melee cap routes.
  • All three paths assume your account systems are fully capped, including Haki, Ascension, Skill Tree, Blessings, Enchants, Artifacts, Tower upgrades, Boss Rush upgrades, and Crystal Defense upgrades.

Best Builds FAQ
What does the Builds page do in Sailor Piece Wiki?
The Builds page shows theoretical endgame cap setups, compares max damage and luck paths, and helps players plan what upgrades to chase next.
Does the build planner use the full game pool?
It is built to include the tracked weapons, melees, powers, titles, races, clans, traits, accessories, auras, runes, artifacts, and more so players can mark what they already own.
Can the build planner suggest the next best upgrade?
Yes. The progression section ranks stronger options by slot so players can see what to farm next based on value and difficulty.
What is the difference between benchmark stats and theoretical cap stats?
A benchmark reflects a real current setup, while the theoretical cap assumes the strongest full setup with top rerolls, maxed systems, and best-in-slot gear.
