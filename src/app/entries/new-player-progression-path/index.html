Overview

New Player Progression Path

  • This page is meant to be a practical roadmap, not a rigid one-build-only rulebook.
  • The level bands are approximate; your real stage depends more on whether you can clear the next boss, afford the next route, and handle the next system grind.
  • Most strong accounts still pass through the same core checkpoints: Dark Blade or another safe starter upgrade, early Haki, artifacts, ascensions, stronger bridge bosses, and then late-game systems such as runes, spec passives, Boss Rush, Infinite Tower, and now Crystal Defense.

How to use this progression page

  • Treat the level bands like checkpoints, not hard walls. If your account already has better drops, you can skip forward. If your build is behind, stay in the earlier phase longer.
  • This route assumes a fully solo, fully F2P account with no boosting, so it prioritizes practical upgrades and systems that keep the whole account moving forward.
  • Later branches depend on what races, clans, titles, and boss drops your storage can support, so the endgame route section is meant to help you branch cleanly instead of forcing one exact final build.
  • If a phase mentions a system or route name that came from the original community layout, use the related pages below for the closest current wiki guide or page alternative.

Phase 1 - Getting started

  • Join the group and claim the starter reward early so your first money and gem curve feels much healthier.
  • Do the Demonite quest as soon as you can, then go to Snow Island and start the Haki side quest so your normal Combat leveling also keeps that quest moving.
  • Progress normally with Combat until the Haki questline is done, then use the currencies you built to buy Dark Blade as your first major safe upgrade.
  • Rebuild currency after Dark Blade and grab both Armament Haki and Observation Haki before you push much farther.
  • Use your rerolls for practical baseline upgrades instead of perfection. An Epic Race, Epic Trait, and even an Uncommon-or-better Clan are enough to keep the phase moving cleanly.
  • Unlock Artifacts around level 2,500, aim for Gryphon after Dark Blade, and start leaning on Gryphon farming once it is online.
  • Once Gryphon is online, focus on leveling speed and simple AFK mob groups like Hollows or Slimes while redeeming any Codes you can use along the way.
Section

Phase 2 - Entering midgame

  • Unlock the major systems as your levels rise: Skill Tree around level 7,500, Spec Passives around level 10,000, and Powers around level 11,500.
  • Check the Stats system on Sailor Island regularly too, because those rerolls and stat grades matter much more once the account stops being purely level-driven.
  • Legendary Race and Trait should start becoming your normal baseline here, and a Rare or Epic Clan is a strong upgrade if you can land one.
  • If your damage is high enough, try to pick up Quake and use the faster Shinjuku-style farming route. If not, keep using Gryphon to AFK efficient mob packs until your account catches up.
  • Farm Boss Keys steadily and start spamming Knight through the Bosses Guide until you can afford Soul Reaper, since that is one of the cleanest midgame upgrades for pushing forward.
  • After Soul Reaper, Shadow becomes the next major stretch target if your account is ready for a longer grind. While climbing, keep practical accessories like Shadow Cloak or E7 Saber Armor in mind.
  • Begin the Rune grind during this stage and keep Ascension requirements visible, because both systems start deciding how smooth the next phase feels.
Section

Phase 3 - Preparation for lategame

  • Before you push the bigger late-game ability grinds, try to reach a stable setup: Legendary-or-better Race and Trait, Rare-or-better Clan, Haki around level 60, and most of the core damage nodes in Skill Tree.
  • A decent damage-focused Spec Passive, a Rare-or-better Power, and at least A+ stat grades make the next ability routes much less painful.
  • Good bridge pieces here include a damage title like Soul Reaper or Eminence in Shadow, an E2 Shadow Cloak or E7 Saber Armor, and useful runes such as Destruction Rune or Wrath Rune.
  • Either Soul Reaper or Shadow should already be on the account by this point, along with Ascension 3 or higher and a respectable Celestial Rupture artifact setup.
  • Once those pieces are in place, start preparing for one of the big late-game ability routes such as Strongest of Today, Strongest in History, or King of Heroes.
  • All three of those routes scale much harder once you can also push their masteries, so the goal here is to arrive ready rather than force the grind too early.
Section

Phase 4 - The strain of lategame

  • After you secure one of the big late-game abilities, go back to upgrading the rest of the account instead of tunnel-visioning only the weapon or melee route.
  • A strong target package here is a Secret Trait, a good Mythical Race, a Legendary Clan, a Legendary-or-better damage Spec Passive, and a Legendary-or-better Power.
  • SS+ stat grades, E5 or better accessories, and stronger rune combinations like Havoc Rune plus Fortune Rune help turn this stage from barely clearable into comfortable farming.
  • You should also be pushing toward a full stronger artifact setup such as high-end Celestial Rupture or Abyssal Crown pieces, along with Ascension 4 or higher.
  • Once that baseline is in place, the next big branch goals become Blessed Maiden, Atomic, or Ice Queen depending on what your account already supports.
  • Ice Queen is the biggest broad power spike of those three for many accounts, so it is worth treating as a priority pickup before the Sea 2 handoff if your route allows it.
Section

Phase 5 - The entrance to new world

  • Once you reach Ascension 5, enter Sea 2 and do the Conqueror Haki quest as soon as possible, because that unlock becomes part of the expected baseline from here forward.
  • Before you fully move into Sea 2, finish the main prep list: secure Conqueror Haki, obtain Ice Queen, and max the important Boss Rush upgrades you can already afford.
  • Push your stats as high as your rerolls allow, ideally into SSS territory, and min-max the systems you already unlocked like Spec Passives, Powers, Artifacts, and Skill Tree.
  • Primordial Rune and Radiant Rune become major targets here, since they are part of many real endgame damage and farming setups.
  • Try to enter Sea 2 with a real E10 accessory plan instead of a temporary filler piece. Great Mage is a practical target if your route lines up with it.
  • This is the point where the account should stop feeling like a bridge build and start behaving like a real late-game setup.
Section

Phase 6 - Path to the strongest

  • Use current tier lists as a reality check for the top metas, but treat them as direction rather than a reason to throw away a nearly finished route that is still carrying your account.
  • True endgame goals here are the best available Trait, Race, Clan, and Bloodline combinations, plus ownership of most top meta weapons or melee routes with their blessings maxed.
  • Full Z stats, maxed Haki, maxed rune investment, and permanent-system completion across Boss Rush, Infinite Tower, Crystal Defense, and Skill Tree all become normal account goals here.
  • Aim for fully upgraded E10 accessories, top titles, min-maxed Artifacts, Powers, and Spec Passives, plus the major chase runes like Primordial Rune and Radiant Rune.
  • The account cap also expands into legendary auras, cosmetics, Relics, and deeper utility systems like Guild upgrades once the main combat shell is already solved.
  • At that point you are no longer just progressing through the game. You are optimizing at the very top.

Big picture rule

  • Do not treat one exact level as the moment you must swap routes. In Sailor Piece, progression is more about what your account can actually clear and afford than the number beside your name.
  • Good accounts snowball by stacking systems together: ascensions, Haki, artifacts, accessories, runes, spec passives, titles, races, clans, and boss upgrades all matter at the same time.
  • If a route is technically available but drags your account backward, use the bridge option first and come back stronger.

New Player Progression Path FAQ
What is the safest early-game progression in Sailor Piece?
The safer route is to build around Combat or Katana into a stable early weapon like Dark Blade, unlock early Haki, keep ascensions moving, and only branch harder once your account can actually support the next boss or material route.
Should new players spin fruits right away?
Usually no. Early fruit gambling can work if you high-roll, but safer progression usually comes from a stable early weapon or spec route that lets you keep money, gems, and rerolls for guaranteed upgrades.
When should I start caring about late-game systems like runes, artifacts, and spec passives?
Artifacts matter surprisingly early once Snow Island opens up, Rune Dungeon becomes worth running in the mid-to-late game, and Spec Passives should become a real priority once your account reaches the published Judgement Island gate and can keep up with the shard grind.
